Chapter Summaries

Chapter 12 — Writing Up the Research Report Ch 12▼
Core Idea

Chapter 12 shows how to assemble all the pieces of your research into a coherent, well-structured report. The report tells a story: why you researched this, how you did it, what you found, and what it means.

Report Structure
  • Abstract — 150–200 words: purpose, method, key finding, conclusion
  • Introduction — background, rationale, research questions (from Proposal)
  • Literature Review — critical synthesis of sources, gap identified
  • Methodology — design, participants, instrument, procedure, ethics
  • Findings — themes or tables/charts with description
  • Discussion — interpret findings; connect to literature; what does it mean?
  • Conclusion — answer your research questions; limitations; future research
  • References — APA 7 reference list
  • Appendices — instrument, consent form, raw data samples
Writing Tips
  • Use academic register: third person, passive where appropriate, hedging language
  • Signpost transitions between sections clearly
  • Every claim in the Discussion must connect to your own data OR to a cited source

Conclusion Chapter — Reflecting on the Process Conclusion▼
Core Idea

Lambert closes by inviting researchers to reflect on what they learned — not just about their topic, but about the research process itself. Limitations and recommendations for future research are essential parts of a conclusion.

What Goes in a Conclusion?
  • Direct answers to your research questions (in clear prose)
  • Limitations: sample size, access, time, instrument validity
  • Recommendations: what should future researchers or practitioners do?
  • Reflection: what would you do differently?

Instructor walks through the full report structure using an example. Groups assemble their three artefacts into a single coherent paper, adding an abstract, discussion, and conclusion.

Day 17 — Final APA Polish

Full-paper reference list audit: every in-text citation must have a reference entry and vice versa. Groups check formatting — hanging indent, sentence case, DOI links, italics. Use the APA Quick-Ref card.

Tue
Aug 4
Day 18 — Presentation Rehearsal

Each group gives a 10-minute dry run. Peers give structured feedback on clarity, time management, and visual design. Instructor gives priority feedback to help groups refine before the real presentation.
