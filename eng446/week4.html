Chapter Summaries

Chapter 8 — Research Designs Ch 8▼
Core Idea

The research design is the blueprint for answering your question. Choosing the right design depends on what you want to know and how you can access it.

Key Designs
  • Survey / questionnaire — gathers data from many participants quickly; best for quantitative questions
  • Interview — explores depth of experience and meaning; best for qualitative questions
  • Observation — records behaviour in natural settings; participant or non-participant
  • Case study — in-depth study of one unit; allows rich, contextualised data
  • Document analysis — analyses existing texts, policies, or records

Chapter 9 — Designing Instruments Ch 9▼
Core Idea

A well-designed instrument collects exactly the data your research question needs. Chapter 9 covers questionnaire design principles and how to write an interview schedule that prompts rich responses.

Questionnaire Design
  • Start with easy, non-threatening questions
  • Mix closed (Likert, multiple choice) and open-ended questions
  • Keep questions short, clear, free of jargon or double meanings
  • Avoid leading questions ("Don't you agree that…")
  • Pilot with 2–3 people before distributing
Interview Schedule
  • Open-ended main questions that invite elaboration
  • Probes: "Can you say more?" / "Can you give an example?"
  • Order from general to specific
  • Closing: "Is there anything else you'd like to add?"
