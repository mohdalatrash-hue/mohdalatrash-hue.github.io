Chapter Summaries

Chapter 6 — Structuring a Literature Review Ch 6▼
Core Idea

A literature review is not a list of summaries — it is a critical synthesis. You group, compare, contrast, and evaluate sources to show what is known, what is debated, and where your gap is.

Three Structures
  • Thematic — organised by recurring themes or concepts (most common)
  • Chronological — traces how the field has developed over time
  • Comparative — places studies side by side to highlight agreements and disagreements
Identifying the Gap

The gap justifies your research: "What has NOT been studied yet that your project will address?" Signal with: "However, limited research has examined…" or "Despite this, no study has…"

Chapter 7 — APA 7 Reference Lists Ch 7▼
Core Idea

Every source cited in-text must appear in the reference list — and vice versa. APA 7 has specific formats for each source type.

Common Formats
  • Journal article: Author, A. A. (Year). Title. Journal, vol(issue), pp. https://doi.org/…
  • Book: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of book. Publisher.
  • Book chapter: Author. (Year). Chapter title. In E. Editor (Ed.), Book title (pp. xx–xx). Publisher.
  • Website: Author. (Year, Date). Page title. Site. URL
Formatting Rules
  • Alphabetical by first author's last name
  • Hanging indent on subsequent lines
  • Only first word of title capitalised (sentence case)
  • Journal names and book titles in italics
