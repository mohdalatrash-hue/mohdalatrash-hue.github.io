Chapter Summaries

Chapter 3 — Research Questions, Aims & Objectives Ch 3▼
Core Idea

A clear, focused research question is the engine of your project. Chapter 3 shows how to move from a broad topic to a specific, feasible question — and how to distinguish aims from objectives.

Key Distinctions
  • Research aim — the broad purpose: what the study is trying to achieve overall
  • Research objectives — specific, numbered steps to achieve the aim
  • Research question — the precise question your data collection will answer
  • Scope — what is and is not included; essential for feasibility
Good vs. Poor Questions
  • ❌ Too broad: "What is the effect of technology on students?"
  • ✅ Focused: "How do Year 2 university students use smartphones during self-study sessions?"

Research aimResearch objectivesResearch questionScopeFeasibility
Chapter 4 — Finding & Evaluating Sources Ch 4▼
Core Idea

A literature review is a critical survey of what is already known. Chapter 4 covers how to search efficiently and how to evaluate whether a source is trustworthy and relevant.

Search Strategies
  • Use Boolean operators: AND, OR, NOT
  • Databases: Google Scholar, ERIC, JSTOR, Scopus
  • Use truncation (*) and phrase searching (" ") for precision
  • Prioritise peer-reviewed articles published within the last 10 years
Evaluating Sources (CRAAP)
  • Currency — how recent?
  • Relevance — addresses your topic?
  • Authority — peer-reviewed?
  • Accuracy — evidence-based?
  • Purpose — objective?

Boolean operatorsPeer reviewPrimary sourceSecondary sourceCRAAP test
Chapter 5 — APA 7 In-Text Citation Ch 5▼
Core Idea

APA 7 uses an author-date in-text system. Every borrowed idea must acknowledge its source; full details go in the reference list.

The Three Moves
  • Paraphrase — restate in your own words: (Lambert, 2012)
  • Summary — condense a longer passage: (Lambert, 2012)
  • Direct quote — exact words in quotation marks: (Lambert, 2012, p. 14)
Common Formats
  • One author: (Smith, 2020) or Smith (2020) argues…
  • Two authors: (Smith & Jones, 2020)
  • Three or more: (Smith et al., 2020)
