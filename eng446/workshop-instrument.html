Instrument Design Workshop

Question type identifier, Likert scale demo, bad-question fixer, and design quiz

Three main question types for a questionnaire: Closed (pre-set answers, easy to analyse quantitatively), Open (free text, rich but harder to analyse), Likert scale (5 or 7-point agreement scale — the most common in EFL research).
Identify the question type
Question type guide
Closed question — gives participants fixed options to choose from
Example: "How often do you use English outside of class? □ Never □ Sometimes □ Often □ Always"
Advantage: Easy to tally and compare. Limitation: Does not capture nuance or unexpected responses.
Likert scale — measures strength of agreement or attitude on a numbered scale
Example: "Peer feedback helps me improve my writing." (1=Strongly Disagree → 5=Strongly Agree)"
Advantage: Captures degree of opinion. Widely used and accepted. Produces ordinal data for statistical analysis.
Open question — participants answer in their own words
Example: "Describe a time when peer feedback was particularly helpful or unhelpful."
Advantage: Rich, unexpected data. Limitation: Harder to analyse — requires thematic coding.
Likert scale tips: Use 5 or 7 points (not 3 or 10). Label both ends AND the midpoint. Keep the scale consistent throughout the questionnaire (don't mix 5-point and 7-point scales). Reverse-score some items to catch inattentive responses.
Try a sample Likert questionnaire

Answer as if you are a student. Notice how the scale captures degree of agreement, not just yes/no.

Likert scale design rules
✓ Do
  • Use 5 or 7 points
  • Label all anchor points clearly
  • Include a neutral midpoint (e.g. "3 = Neither")
  • Pilot the questionnaire with 2–3 peers first
  • Group items by theme
✗ Don't
  • Use double-barrelled items ("I enjoy and benefit from peer feedback")
  • Use leading language ("Don't you agree that…")
  • Make all items positively worded (include some reversed)
  • Use too many items (20 max for questionnaires)
Common questionnaire design errors: Double-barrelled questions, leading questions, ambiguous wording, jargon, and yes/no items where a scale is needed. Click "Reveal fix" to see how each should be corrected.
