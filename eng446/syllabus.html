Course Description

ENG 446 introduces students to the principles and practice of educational research. Working through Lambert (2012), students learn to formulate a research question, select an appropriate methodology, collect and analyse data, and present findings in the conventions of academic writing. The course culminates in a group research paper developed across three submitted artefacts (Proposal, Literature Review, and Methodology & Analysis) and an individual final examination.

Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)
CLOOn successful completion, students will be able to:
CLO 1Define research, distinguish research types (qualitative, quantitative, mixed), and identify characteristics of rigorous inquiry.
CLO 2Explain major research paradigms (positivism, interpretivism, critical) and their implications for research design and ethics.
CLO 3Construct a focused, feasible research proposal with a clear title, research questions, and rationale.
CLO 4Locate, evaluate, synthesise, and cite academic sources in APA 7th edition format.
CLO 5Design appropriate data-collection instruments (questionnaires, interview guides) aligned with the chosen methodology.
CLO 6Analyse data using basic qualitative (thematic analysis) or quantitative (descriptive statistics) techniques.
CLO 7Write and present a coherent research report following academic conventions, demonstrating validity, reliability, and ethical compliance.

Course Policies

📝 Assessments

  • Quizzes are taken in class via Blackboard Ultra — no make-up quizzes.
  • Group artefacts are submitted on Blackboard. All group members must be listed on submissions.
  • Late submissions lose 10% per day unless a medical or official excuse is submitted in advance.
  • The final exam is closed-book. All chapters (Intro–Ch12) and key terms are examinable.

👥 Group Work

  • Groups are assigned by the instructor on Day 1 — you cannot change groups.
  • All group members receive the same grade for group artefacts.
  • Any student not contributing may be separated and given an individual project (instructor's discretion).
  • All submitted work must be original. Plagiarism results in zero for the component and referral to the dean.

📱 Technology

  • Phones allowed for Blackboard quizzes and reference to this study site only.
  • Laptops welcome for studio sessions.
  • AI writing tools must be declared; undeclared use is treated as plagiarism.

📖 Academic Integrity

  • Cite all sources using APA 7th edition — see the APA Quick-Ref card.
  • Paraphrase rather than copy; when quoting directly, use quotation marks and a page number.
  • All work submitted must be your own (or your group's) original work.

📚 Study Resources

All study materials are on this site — no login required. Reading guides, chapter summaries, practice question banks, and APA reference cards are open from any device. Grades and official submissions are in Blackboard Ultra only.
