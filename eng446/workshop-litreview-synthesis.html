Literature Review: Synthesis & Build

Stop listing sources and start connecting them — build a synthesis matrix, weave citations, and turn your gap into a research question.

🔧 From a pile of sources to a synthesised review

The hardest part of a literature review is moving from "what each source says" to "what the sources say together". This workshop gives you the tools your group can apply directly to its own reading.

A synthesis matrix maps sources (rows) against themes (columns). Where two or more sources meet on a theme, you have material to synthesise. Click the cells to mark which source addresses which theme, then press the button under each theme.

The gap is the bridge to your research question. A well-formed RQ answers exactly the gap the review identified — same topic, same population, now framed as an open inquiry.
