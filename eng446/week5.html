Chapter Summaries

Chapter 10 — Qualitative Analysis Ch 10▼
Core Idea

Qualitative analysis makes sense of words, images, and non-numerical data by identifying patterns and meanings. Thematic analysis is the most accessible approach for beginner researchers.

Thematic Analysis Steps
  • Step 1 — Familiarise: Read and re-read your data; note initial ideas
  • Step 2 — Code: Label segments with a short descriptive word or phrase
  • Step 3 — Search for themes: Group related codes into potential themes
  • Step 4 — Review themes: Check themes against the full dataset; refine
  • Step 5 — Define & name: Write a clear definition for each theme
  • Step 6 — Write up: Report themes with illustrative quotations from your data

Chapter 11 — Quantitative Analysis Ch 11▼
Core Idea

Quantitative analysis summarises and describes numerical data using statistics. At beginner level, descriptive statistics are sufficient — you describe what the data shows, rather than inferring beyond your sample.

Key Descriptive Statistics
  • Frequency — how many times each response occurred; expressed as a count or percentage
  • Mean — the arithmetic average; sum all values ÷ number of values
  • Mode — the most frequently occurring value
  • Range — highest value minus lowest value; shows spread
Presenting Data
  • Use tables for exact numbers and frequency distributions
  • Use bar charts or pie charts to show proportions visually
  • Always label axes, include units, and title every figure
