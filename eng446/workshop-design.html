Research Design Workshop

Paradigm questionnaire, method-to-question matching, and design identification quiz

Research paradigm = your philosophical stance on how knowledge is produced. Three paradigms matter for ENG 446: Positivist (objective reality, measurable, quantitative), Interpretivist (subjective experience, meaning-making, qualitative), Constructivist (knowledge built through interaction and context). Your paradigm shapes every other design decision.
Paradigm Questionnaire — for your group's research

Answer these 6 questions based on your group's research topic and goals. Your paradigm will be revealed at the end.

The method must fit the question. If your RQ asks "how many?" → quantitative. If it asks "why?" or "how do people experience?" → qualitative. If you need both → mixed methods. Select the best method for each research question below.
Match each research question to the best method
Research design decision tree
Does your RQ ask about amounts, frequencies, or relationships between measurable variables?
→ Quantitative (survey with Likert scale, statistical analysis)
Does your RQ ask about experiences, perceptions, meanings, or processes?
→ Qualitative (semi-structured interviews, observation, document analysis)
Do you need both breadth (numbers) and depth (meaning)?
→ Mixed methods (survey + follow-up interviews; analyse numbers first, then explain with words)
Do you want to study one case in rich detail?
→ Case study (can be qualitative or mixed; good for bounded contexts like one classroom)
