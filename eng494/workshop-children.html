🧸 What makes children's translation special

The translator juggles three things at once — source words, target words, and pictures (Hoole noticed this tension back in 1659). Add onomatopoeia, signs drawn inside images, and a child-friendly voice that must still be read aloud well.

📖
Required reading for this workshop: “Multimediality, Onomatopoeia and Inscriptions.” The exercises below practise sound-words, nonsense and word–image interplay from this reading.
Multimodality: text and image make meaning together. Watch for: text the picture contradicts or completes, words drawn inside the picture (signs, labels), and cultural/visual layout (including right-to-left reading order in Arabic).
Onomatopoeia — words that imitate sound — rarely map one-to-one. English splash, buzz, knock have conventional Arabic counterparts you must know or coin, keeping them readable aloud for a child.
