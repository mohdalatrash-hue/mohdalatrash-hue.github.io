Unit 1 · Adult Prose

Dialect, Tone & Register

Hear the voice before you translate it — identify register and dialect, choose a strategy, then translate real vernacular excerpts into Arabic.

🎙️ Why this matters

Landers argues that nearly every word sits in a register (formal/informal, technical, regional, vulgar/polite) and that dialect is among the translator's hardest problems. You cannot reproduce English phonetic spelling in Arabic — so you must compensate. This workshop trains the ear, then your pen.

Register is a socially-defined variety of language. Categories include: formal/informal, technical/non-technical, standard/regional, jargon, and proper/vulgar. The first job is to name the register you hear.
Dialect signals region, class, era and character. Authors mark it with phonetic spelling and slang. Identify the variety — and the specific feature that will fight you in Arabic.
Now translate. Render each short excerpt into natural Arabic. Type your version, then reveal the translator's notes and a self-assessment checklist. There is no single right answer — judge yours against the criteria.
Four moves for hard spots (Landers): a footnote explains; an interpolation slips a brief gloss into the text; an omission drops the untranslatable; compensation recreates the effect elsewhere (e.g. a colloquial Arabic register for English dialect).
